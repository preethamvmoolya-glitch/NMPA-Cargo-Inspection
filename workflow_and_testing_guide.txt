1. SYSTEM WORKFLOW & OPERATIONS PIPELINE (WHAT COMES AFTER WHAT)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The platform secures and automates cargo clearances through the following pipeline:

Step 1: Onboarding & Configuration (System Admin)
        - Admin manages operator registrations (Inspectors & Port Authorities) 
          and sets the global weight discrepancy tolerance threshold (e.g., 5.0%).

Step 2: Manifest Registration & AI RMS Evaluation (Inspector)
        - Frontline gate inspectors log the Incoming Vessel Manifest (IGM). 
        - The system triggers the AI Risk Management System (RMS) to assign an
          initial risk rating (Routine Risk, Elevated Risk, Critical Risk) based 
          on cargo description, port of origin, and tonnage.
        - Manifest enters the queue as "Awaiting Physical Inspection".

Step 3: Weighbridge Physical Check & Verification (Inspector)
        - The container arrives at the weighbridge. The inspector records the 
          actual physical weight, seal integrity status, structural damage status, 
          and uploads photographic proof.
        - Cargo status transitions to "Inspected - Awaiting Authority Adjudication".

Step 4: Clearance Adjudication & QR Gate Pass Generation (Port Authority)
        - Port Authority reviews the manifest. The system flags a warning if the 
          declared weight vs. weighbridge weight discrepancy exceeds the admin's threshold.
        - Authority decides to:
          a) Approve: Grants clearance, generating a unique QR Verification Pass.
          b) Reject/Detain: Detains cargo for audit.
          c) Re-Inspect: Resets cargo status to "Pending" and wipes physical checks.

Step 5: QR Code Verification Registry (Public Gate Pass Portal)
        - Security gates scan the QR Code printed on the Gate Pass (or access the 
          public URL with the verification token) to verify authenticity.

Step 6: Grievance Redressal Portal (Public Page)
        - Operators, employers, or the public submit formal grievances. Critical 
          reports are escalated directly to the NMPA Chairman's Office.
        - System Admin reviews these queues from the Admin Console.
